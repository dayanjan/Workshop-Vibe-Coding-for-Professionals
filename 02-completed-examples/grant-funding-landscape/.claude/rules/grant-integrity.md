## Grant Data Integrity Rules

- NEVER fabricate preliminary data, citations, or study results. All data points must trace back to a verifiable public source.
- NEVER guarantee funding outcomes or quote success rates as predictions for a specific application.
- Always verify that referenced Funding Opportunity Announcements (FOAs) are current and have not expired. Check the expiration date before recommending any FOA.
- Distinguish strategic suggestions from eligibility requirements. Use language like "consider" or "one approach is" for strategy, and "required" or "must" only for actual NIH policy constraints.
- State career stage and institutional support assumptions explicitly. A strategy that works for an ESI at an R1 university does not apply to a mid-career investigator at a community hospital.
- All funding data must come from public sources: NIH Reporter, NIH Data Book, institute strategic plans, or published FOAs.
- Always cite the fiscal year and data retrieval date when presenting funding statistics. NIH budgets shift annually, and stale numbers mislead investigators.
